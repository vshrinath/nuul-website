Questions

Frequently asked

Is this really like having an executive assistant? ▾
That's exactly how to think about it. A great EA remembers your preferences, knows your projects and the people in them, organises your chaos, and shows up ready with context when you need it. nuul does all of this — automatically, privately, and without the overhead of managing another person. The difference is nuul gets better the more you use it, works 24/7, and costs almost nothing to run.
Do I need to be technical to use nuul? ▾
Not at all. Setup takes about ten minutes. If you can use Google Docs, you can use nuul. We're building the setup to be as simple as possible — and a full how-it-works guide is coming soon for anyone who wants to go deeper.
Who can see my data? ▾
Nobody but you. Your vault is stored in files you own and control — not locked inside a vendor's database. nuul never trains on your data, sells it, or accesses it without your knowledge. Whatever hosting option you choose, that guarantee never changes. And if nuul disappeared tomorrow, your files are yours in plain, readable format — complete and accessible forever.
Does nuul replace my AI subscription? ▾
No — nuul makes your AI subscriptions dramatically more useful. Think of it as the memory layer your Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini is missing. nuul passes your accumulated context to whichever model you use, so every session starts with the full picture instead of a blank slate.
What if I want to switch AI models? ▾
Just change one setting. Your entire operational memory — notes, people, threads, decisions — travels to the new model instantly. The context is stored as plain files, not inside any AI provider's systems. Switch freely, any time.
How is this different from Notion AI or ChatGPT memory? ▾
Notion AI works on documents but doesn't compound across time or connect conversations, voice notes, and WhatsApp messages. ChatGPT memory is limited, vendor-controlled, and disappears if you switch models or plans. nuul gives you an open, growing, structurally-linked memory that works with any model — including ones that don't exist yet.
